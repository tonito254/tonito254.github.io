History of Kibwezi

  • 1900: Kibwezi was founded as a small rural settlement.
  • 1920s: The British colonial government established Kibwezi as a major railway stop along the Uganda Railway.
  • 1930s: Kibwezi became a popular trading center for local farmers and merchants.
  • 1940s: Kibwezi was a significant center of activity during World War II, with the British Army setting up camps and airfields in the area.
  • 1960s: Kibwezi was a key site of resistance during the struggle for Kenyan independence, with local activists participating in protests and acts of civil disobedience.
  • 1970s: Kibwezi experienced a period of growth and development as the Kenyan government invested in infrastructure and public services in the region.
  • 1980s: Kibwezi continued to develop, with new schools, health clinics, and other public facilities being built in the town.
  • 1990s: Kibwezi became a major transportation hub for the region, with a new highway and expanded railway facilities bringing increased economic activity and investment to the area.
  • 2000s: Kibwezi continued to grow and modernize, with new commercial and residential developments being built in the town.
  • 2010s: Kibwezi became a key center of activity for the proposed Lamu Port-South Sudan-Ethiopia Transport (LAPSSET) corridor project, with plans for a new railway line and other infrastructure to be built in the area.

    LifeinKibwezi is a website created by Muiva and Mulinge, aimed at capturing and documenting the diverse and beautiful town of Kibwezi. The website showcases the gradual growth of Kibwezi through generations, as seen through the lens of skilled photographers. Our goal is to highlight the unique characteristics and attributes of Kibwezi, from its natural beauty to its rich culture and history. We hope that our collection of photographs will not only be visually stunning, but also serve as a tribute to the people and places that make Kibwezi so special.
